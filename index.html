Locations & Attractions Verified

Ginkgo Gem Shop (Vantage, WA)

Real roadside attraction at 330 Ginkgo Ave, near Ginkgo Petrified Forest State Park. Dinosaur statues confirmed. Note: Opens at 10:30 AM — may not be open for an 8:45 AM arrival. Plan for exterior viewing only.

Atlas Mill Waterfront Park (CDA)

Real 12.5-acre park (officially “Atlas Mill Park”) along the Spokane River with 4,000 ft of waterfront, kayak launch, dog park, swim area, and playground. May be closer to Exit 12 or 15 than Exit 11 — verify driving directions.

Wingate by Wyndham Butte

Confirmed at 2107 Cornell Ave, Butte, MT 59701. 74-room hotel with free breakfast, indoor pool, fitness center, and free parking.

Yellowstone Thermal Features

Most attractions verified: Norris Geyser Basin, Mammoth Hot Springs, Fountain Paint Pot, Grand Prismatic Overlook (Fairy Falls trailhead, ~0.6–0.8 mi one-way with 105 ft elevation gain — not completely flat), Old Faithful (~90 min avg eruption cycle), Artists Paint Pots (1 mi loop), Canyon viewpoints (Artist Point views Lower Falls only), Mud Volcano & Dragon’s Mouth Spring (same thermal area), West Thumb (Fishing Cone in lake water). Biscuit Basin closed indefinitely since July 2024 hydrothermal explosion.

Grand Teton Locations

All confirmed: Glacier View Turnout (views 3 glaciers), Snake River Overlook (Ansel Adams photo spot — note trees now partially block historic view), Elk Ranch Flats (prime wildlife), Jackson Lake Lodge (60-ft panoramic windows, free public lobby, Lunch Tree Hill picnic nearby), Oxbow Bend (iconic mirror reflections of Mt. Moran), Schwabacher Landing (beaver pond reflections), Mormon Row (Moulton Barns, National Register of Historic Places), Jenny Lake Shuttle Boat ($20 adult RT, runs every 10–15 min, 7 AM–7 PM peak season), Hidden Falls & Inspiration Point, String Lake (shallow, warmest lake in park, ~50s°F in early July), Signal Mountain (driveable 5-mi summit road).

Shoshone Falls & Perrine Bridge

Shoshone Falls: 212 ft, wider than Niagara (“Niagara of the West”). ~6 mi from I-84 (10-min detour). Perrine Bridge: 486 ft above canyon floor, only US structure allowing year-round no-permit BASE jumping. Both in Twin Falls, ID.

Victor Emporium / Prodigal Son Brewery

Victor Emporium: Real since 1950, 45 N Main St. Famous for huckleberry milkshakes (featured on Food Network). Prodigal Son Brewery: 230 SE Court Ave, Pendleton — #1 rated restaurant in Pendleton on TripAdvisor. Open Wed–Sat.

Warehouse Food Hall / Radisson Pendleton

Warehouse Food Hall: Real, 370 S 8th St, Boise. 29,000+ sq ft, 13 kitchens, 2 bars. ~2–4 blocks from Julia Davis Park. Radisson Hotel Pendleton Airport: Confirmed at 4343 NW A Ave, 0.1 mi from airport.

Yellow Church Cafe — PERMANENTLY CLOSED

The Yellow Church Cafe in Ellensburg is permanently closed. The building at 111 S Pearl St is now occupied by La Santa Mexican Cuisine. Alternative Ellensburg dining options should be researched before the trip.

Olmstead Place & SR-821 Scenic Byway

Olmstead Place: 217-acre state park, 1875 cottonwood log cabin (still standing), 0.75-mi Altapes Creek trail. SR-821: Confirmed 25-mi scenic byway from Exit 26, basalt cliffs rising ~2,000 ft, densest nesting raptors in WA state.

Weather Validation (Late June / Early July)

Yellowstone Mornings (32–48°F)

Accurate. Historical data: Yellowstone Lake avg low 34°F, Mammoth avg low 42°F in June. Freezing temps common at higher elevations. Thermal steam visibility is excellent.

Yellowstone Midday (60–70°F)

Mostly accurate. Yellowstone Lake avg high 62°F, Mammoth avg high 70°F. Most of the park averages 62–66°F; the 70°F upper end only at lower-elevation spots like Mammoth. UV is intense; sunscreen essential.

Butte, MT Evening (50s°F)

Overstated. At 5,538 ft elevation, Butte’s June average low is 41°F, July low ~45°F. Evening sunset temps may briefly hit upper 40s–low 50s, but expect 40s°F overnight. Pack warmer layers than the itinerary suggests.

Grand Teton (38–46°F morning / 68–73°F midday)

Mornings accurate (avg low 39–45°F). Midday slightly low — avg highs reach 75–82°F in early July, especially in Jackson Hole valley. Plan for warmer hiking conditions.

Eastern WA/ID (74–78°F)

Too low for most of the route. Spokane avg high ~74°F but Tri-Cities/Walla Walla reach 79–86°F in late June. Columbia Basin and lower-elevation areas routinely exceed 80°F. Pack for 85–95°F at lower elevations.

Boise, ID (78–83°F)

Significantly underestimated. Boise’s July avg high is 92.7°F. Early July daily highs range 88–93°F, with 100°F+ possible (6 days/year on average). The claimed 78–83°F is off by ~10°F. Bring ample water, sun protection, and plan for genuine heat.

Pendleton, OR & Ellensburg, WA (72–76°F)

Significantly underestimated. Pendleton July avg high: 84–90°F. Ellensburg July avg high: 82–87°F. The claimed 72–76°F is off by 10–15°F. These are hot, semi-arid locations in early July. Pack accordingly.

Advisories & Recommendations

Day 3: Biscuit Basin CLOSED

Biscuit Basin has been closed indefinitely since a hydrothermal explosion on July 23, 2024 that destroyed portions of the boardwalk. Small explosions have continued. As of June 2026, no reopening date announced. Remove this stop and spend extra time at Grand Prismatic or Old Faithful area.

Day 7: Major Distance/Time Error

Tetonia to Twin Falls is 225–233 miles, requiring 3.5–4 hours (not the claimed 2h 15m / ~180 mi). The total Day 7 distance is ~570–590 miles (not ~340). This makes Day 7 a very long 9–10 hour driving day. Consider: departing Tetonia by 7:30–8:00 AM instead of 9:30 AM, or cutting the Shoshone Falls stop.

Day 1: Seattle–Vantage–CDA Timing

Seattle to Vantage is underestimated by ~15 min (136 mi, ~2h 30m). Vantage to CDA is underestimated by ~45 min (177 mi, ~3h 15m). Combined, Day 1 runs ~1 hour behind schedule. Expect Butte arrival closer to 8:00 PM. Also: time zone change occurs at Lookout Pass (ID/MT border), not at St. Regis itself.

Day 2: Lamar Valley Return Route

The in-park North Loop totals ~168 miles of driving. After Lamar Valley, returning to West Gate requires driving back across the entire park (~65 mi, 1.5–2h), plus “bison jams” that can add 30–60 min. The “30 min to Island Park” only applies from West Gate. Plan for 7:00–7:30 PM cabin arrival.

Day 6: Sunrise Timing

Sunrise on July 2 at Grand Teton is ~5:54 AM MDT. The 4:45 AM Tetonia departure arrives ~6:00–6:05 AM at Schwabacher Landing — slightly after sunrise. Depart by 4:15–4:30 AM to catch first light. Also verify Teton Pass road conditions (landslide repairs completed July 2025).

Day 8: Yellow Church Cafe Closed

The Yellow Church Cafe in Ellensburg is permanently closed. The building now houses La Santa Mexican Cuisine. Research alternative Ellensburg dining options before the trip. Olmstead Place State Park and SR-821 byway are both confirmed excellent.

Yellowstone Peak Season Strategy

Late June is peak season. The 7:00–7:30 AM West Entrance entries are smart — expect 15–45 min gate waits if later. Old Faithful parking fills by 10 AM. The Gardiner–Mammoth road was damaged by 2022 flooding — check NPS road status before travel. Also note Ginkgo Gem Shop opens at 10:30 AM (Day 1 arrival at 8:45 AM may be exterior-only).

July 4th Return & Weather Prep

I-90 westbound sees heavy July 4th return traffic in the afternoon. Budget an extra 30 min buffer. Critical: Boise, Pendleton, and Ellensburg temps will be 10–15°F hotter than the itinerary claims (expect upper 80s to 90s). Carry ample water and sun protection for Days 7–8.
